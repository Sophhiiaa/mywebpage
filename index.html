Mummy's Been Making

Mummy's Been Making began years ago with my daughter because every time I baked or crafted or anything like that she would exclaim 'MUMMY'S BEEN MAKING!'. Pyrography has always been in the back of my mind since I first attempted it in the UK over seven years ago; but because I had never heard that you could use transfer it didn't really work out. However four years ago I gave pyrography another go, but this time it did work out. Originally, I used a brass tip pen, which I use still, but now I more often use my razor tip 'Peter Child'. My preferred wood to use would probably be beech, but I also use maranti and pine wood. Treated wood such as: painted; waxed or oiled wood is un-useable because when heated the fumes are very toxic. However once finished burning, I can then apply my trusty food-safe oil, wax or varnish on your boards and designs.
